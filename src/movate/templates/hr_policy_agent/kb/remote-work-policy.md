# Remote Work & Hybrid Schedule Policy

**Effective date:** January 1, 2025
**Applies to:** All full-time employees in hybrid-eligible roles
**Policy owner:** People Operations & Workplace Experience
**Questions:** workplace@company.com

---

## 1. Hybrid Work Model

The company operates on a **hybrid work model** for the majority of roles. All employees
in hybrid-eligible positions are required to be physically present in their assigned
office a **minimum of 3 days per week**. The company's designated anchor days —
the days all hybrid employees are expected to be in the office — are:

- **Tuesday**
- **Wednesday**
- **Thursday**

Employees may work from home on Mondays and Fridays. Anchor days exist to ensure
cross-team collaboration, in-person mentorship, and team cohesion. Managers may not
excuse employees from anchor days on a recurring basis without HR review. Individual
exceptions for specific anchor days (e.g., travel, illness) should be communicated
to the manager in advance.

---

## 2. Work-From-Home Eligibility

Not all roles are eligible for hybrid or remote work. To qualify for the hybrid
schedule (home on Mondays and Fridays), all of the following criteria must be met:

1. **Role eligibility:** The role must not require regular in-person client-facing
   interaction, physical presence for laboratory or hardware work, or other duties
   that inherently require on-site presence.
2. **Manager approval:** The employee's direct manager must formally approve hybrid
   eligibility in Rippling. New hires in hybrid-eligible roles are automatically
   approved.
3. **Dedicated workspace:** The employee must have a dedicated home workspace that
   provides: reliable, private internet connectivity with a minimum upload and download
   speed of **25 Mbps**; a professional background or virtual background capability
   for video calls; and freedom from persistent household interruptions during
   scheduled working hours.

Failure to maintain these conditions may result in a manager requiring the employee to
increase in-office presence until conditions are remedied.

---

## 3. Home Office Equipment Stipend

The company provides financial support for employees setting up a home workspace:

- **One-time new-hire stipend:** $1,500, available upon the employee's start date,
  for home office furniture, monitor, peripherals, and accessories
- **Annual refresh allowance:** $500 per calendar year for accessories, upgrades,
  or replacements (chair cushion, keyboard, webcam, etc.)
- **Company-provided computer:** The primary work laptop is provided by the company
  and is **not** covered by or counted against the stipend amounts
- **Expense submission:** All stipend expenses must be submitted through **Expensify**
  with itemized receipts within 30 days of purchase; expenses are reimbursed within
  one payroll cycle after approval
- **Ineligible expenses:** Personal furniture (non-desk/chair), appliances,
  smartphones, tablets (non-work-use), and internet service subscriptions

Stipend funds are use-it-or-lose-it within the calendar year for the annual refresh
allowance; unused amounts do not roll over. The new-hire stipend expires 6 months from
the start date.

---

## 4. Timezone Requirements

To maintain effective collaboration, all employees must work within a timezone that
keeps them within **3 hours of their team's anchor office timezone**. For example,
an employee whose team is based in Pacific Time (PT) must work from a timezone no
earlier than ET (Eastern Time, UTC-5) and no later than PT (UTC-8).

Requests to work from a timezone outside this 3-hour window require:
1. **VP-level approval** from the employee's reporting chain
2. **HR documentation** of the arrangement, including expected duration and
   any accommodations for overlap hours

Timezone exceptions are reviewed annually and may be revoked if collaboration
effectiveness is impacted.

---

## 5. Fully Remote Positions

A limited number of positions are designated as **"remote-ok"** and do not require
any regular in-office presence. These positions are explicitly labeled as remote-ok
on the job description at the time of posting.

- **Headcount cap:** The company maintains a cap of **15% of total headcount** for
  fully remote employees. Once this cap is reached, new remote-ok requisitions require
  executive approval.
- **Relocation:** Employees in remote-ok roles who relocate to a different state must
  notify HR at least 30 days before the move. Relocation may trigger re-evaluation of
  remote eligibility due to state-specific tax, payroll, and labor law requirements.
  Some states require prior company authorization before an employee may work there.
- **Role reclassification:** A fully remote employee whose role is reclassified to
  hybrid (e.g., due to team restructuring or role changes) will receive a minimum of
  90 days' notice before in-office expectations take effect.

---

## 6. Temporary Work-From-Home Arrangements

Employees in hybrid roles who need to work fully from home for a limited period (e.g.,
temporary medical recovery, caregiver responsibilities, home repair situations) may
request a **temporary WFH arrangement** of up to **4 consecutive weeks per calendar year**.

Requirements:
- Written request submitted to direct manager at least 5 business days in advance
  (or as soon as practicable for emergencies)
- **Manager and HR approval** required; approval is not guaranteed
- The arrangement does not change the employee's permanent employment classification
  or hybrid status
- Extensions beyond 4 weeks require additional HR review and may require FMLA or
  other leave documentation depending on the underlying reason

---

## 7. International Remote Work

Working from outside the United States — even temporarily — creates potential tax nexus,
employment law, and data security obligations for the company. The following rules apply:

- Employees may **not work internationally for more than 14 consecutive days** without
  a prior **Global Mobility review** conducted by HR and Legal
- Employees must **notify HR at least 10 business days before** any international work
  arrangement, including business travel where work will be conducted
- Working internationally without prior approval may trigger unintended tax and legal
  obligations and is grounds for disciplinary action
- The Global Mobility review process typically takes 5–10 business days; do not book
  international work arrangements contingent on approval before the review is complete

---

## 8. Equipment Return

All company-owned equipment (laptop, external monitor provided by IT, company-issued
accessories) must be returned to the company within **10 business days** of the
employee's last day of employment, regardless of reason for departure (resignation,
termination, layoff, or retirement).

- IT will provide a prepaid FedEx return label for remote employees via email on
  the final day of employment
- Equipment that is not returned within 10 business days will result in a **payroll
  deduction** at the equipment's fair market value, as permitted by applicable state law
- Employees are responsible for any damage to company equipment beyond normal wear and tear
