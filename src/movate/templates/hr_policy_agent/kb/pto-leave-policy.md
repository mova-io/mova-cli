# Paid Time Off & Leave Policy

**Effective date:** January 1, 2025
**Applies to:** All full-time and part-time employees in the United States
**Policy owner:** People Operations

---

## 1. Paid Time Off (PTO) Accrual

The company provides a tiered PTO accrual system based on length of service. PTO begins
accruing on the employee's first day of employment and is available for use as it accrues —
there is no waiting period before using accrued PTO.

| Years of Service | Annual PTO Days | Monthly Accrual Rate |
|-----------------|-----------------|----------------------|
| 0–2 years        | 15 days          | 1.25 days/month       |
| 2–5 years        | 20 days          | 1.67 days/month       |
| 5+ years         | 25 days          | 2.08 days/month       |

Years of service are calculated from the employee's most recent hire date. Part-time
employees (scheduled for fewer than 30 hours per week) accrue PTO on a prorated basis
proportional to their scheduled hours relative to a 40-hour full-time week. Contractors,
temporary workers, and interns are not eligible for PTO accrual under this policy.

---

## 2. PTO Carryover and Forfeiture

Employees may carry over a maximum of **5 days (40 hours)** of unused PTO from one
calendar year to the next. Any PTO balance in excess of 5 days as of **December 31**
will be automatically forfeited and will not be paid out. Employees are strongly encouraged
to use their PTO throughout the year. Managers should not unreasonably deny PTO requests
that would otherwise result in forfeiture.

PTO is **not paid out upon resignation or termination** except where required by applicable
state law (e.g., California, Illinois, Massachusetts). Employees in those states should
contact HR for state-specific terms.

---

## 3. PTO Request Process

Employees must submit all PTO requests through the **Rippling HR system** prior to
taking leave. The following notice requirements apply:

- **1–4 consecutive days:** Minimum 3 business days' advance notice; manager discretion
- **5 or more consecutive days:** Minimum **2 weeks' advance notice** required
- **Emergency or unexpected leave:** Notify your manager as soon as reasonably possible;
  document in Rippling retroactively upon return

Managers are required to approve or deny PTO requests within **5 business days** of
submission. Denials must include a written explanation in Rippling. Employees may escalate
unresolved denials to HR after 5 business days.

---

## 4. PTO Blackout Periods

Certain high-demand business periods are designated as PTO blackout periods during which
PTO requests may be denied. Current blackout periods are:

- **December 20 – January 2**: Company-wide year-end close; applies to all employees
- **Last 3 business days of each fiscal quarter (March, June, September, December)**:
  Applies to Finance, Accounting, Revenue Operations, and Customer Success teams only

Requests submitted during blackout periods will not automatically be denied — they will
be reviewed by the manager and HR. However, employees should plan accordingly.

---

## 5. Sick Leave

All employees receive **5 days (40 hours) of sick leave** per calendar year. Sick leave
is granted at the start of each calendar year (not accrued monthly) and does **not**
carry over to the following year. Unused sick leave is forfeited on December 31.

Sick leave may be used for:
- The employee's own illness, injury, or medical appointment
- Care for an immediate family member (spouse, child, parent, or domestic partner)
  who is ill or injured

Employees who exhaust their sick leave may use accrued PTO for additional sick time.
Documentation (doctor's note) may be required for absences exceeding 3 consecutive days.

---

## 6. Parental Leave

The company provides generous paid parental leave to support employees welcoming a child
through birth, adoption, or foster placement.

- **Primary caregiver:** 16 weeks of fully paid leave
- **Secondary caregiver:** 4 weeks of fully paid leave

Parental leave applies equally to biological parents, adoptive parents, and foster parents.
Employees must have completed **90 days of continuous service** to be eligible. Leave
must be taken within **12 months of the birth, adoption, or placement date**. Leave
may be taken continuously or intermittently (minimum 1-week blocks for intermittent use),
subject to manager and HR approval.

Parental leave runs **concurrently with FMLA** where applicable. Employees should notify
HR at least 30 days in advance when the leave date is foreseeable.

---

## 7. Bereavement Leave

Employees are eligible for paid bereavement leave upon the death of a family member:

- **Immediate family:** 5 paid business days
  - Immediate family is defined as: spouse or domestic partner, child (including
    stepchildren and adopted children), parent (including stepparents), and sibling
    (including step-siblings)
- **Extended family:** 2 paid business days
  - Extended family is defined as: grandparent, grandchild, aunt, uncle, niece,
    nephew, or in-laws (parent-in-law, sibling-in-law)

Bereavement leave must be taken within 2 weeks of the date of death. Employees may
request additional unpaid leave or use accrued PTO for travel or estate matters; such
requests are subject to manager approval.

---

## 8. Floating Holidays

Each employee receives **2 floating holidays per calendar year** to observe personal,
cultural, or religious holidays not covered by the company's standard holiday schedule.
Floating holidays must be used within the calendar year in which they are granted and
**do not carry over** to the following year. Unused floating holidays are forfeited
on December 31. Floating holidays are not subject to the standard PTO carryover cap —
they are tracked separately in Rippling.

---

## 9. Family and Medical Leave (FMLA)

Eligible employees may take up to **12 weeks of unpaid, job-protected leave** per year
under the federal Family and Medical Leave Act (FMLA) for qualifying reasons, including:

- Serious health condition of the employee
- Serious health condition of an immediate family member (spouse, child, parent)
- Birth, adoption, or foster placement of a child
- Qualifying military exigency

To be eligible, employees must have worked for the company for at least **12 months**
and logged at least **1,250 hours** in the preceding 12-month period. FMLA leave runs
concurrently with paid parental leave where both apply. Employees must provide 30 days'
notice for foreseeable leave, or as soon as practicable for unforeseeable events.

HR must be notified at the start of any leave that may qualify as FMLA, regardless of
whether the employee requests FMLA designation. Certification from a healthcare provider
is required within 15 calendar days of the leave request.
