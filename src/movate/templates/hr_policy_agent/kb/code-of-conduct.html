Employee Code of Conduct

Last updated: March 15, 2025  |  Owner: People Operations & Legal  |  Version: 4.2  |  View history

1. Overview and Zero-Tolerance Policy

This Code of Conduct establishes the behavioral standards all employees, contractors, and vendors are expected to uphold while working for or on behalf of the company. Our goal is a workplace where every person feels respected, safe, and empowered to do their best work.

Zero-Tolerance Policy: The company maintains a zero-tolerance policy toward harassment, discrimination, retaliation, and workplace violence. Any substantiated violation may result in immediate termination, regardless of the employee's seniority, performance history, or length of service.

Zero-tolerance categories include, but are not limited to: sexual harassment, racial discrimination, physical assault or threats of violence, retaliation against employees who make good-faith reports, and any conduct that creates a hostile work environment based on a protected characteristic.

2. Harassment and Discrimination

2.1 Definition of Harassment

Harassment is unwelcome conduct — verbal, written, electronic, or physical — that is based on a protected characteristic and that either: (a) results in a tangible employment action (quid pro quo harassment), or (b) is sufficiently severe or pervasive to create a hostile, abusive, or intimidating work environment (hostile work environment harassment).

Examples of harassing conduct include, but are not limited to:

  • Offensive jokes, slurs, epithets, or name-calling based on a protected characteristic
  • Unwanted touching, groping, or physical intimidation
  • Displaying or sharing offensive images, memes, or content
  • Persistent unwanted romantic or sexual advances
  • Threatening, demeaning, or humiliating language — verbal, written, or electronic
  • Interfering with an employee's ability to perform their job

2.2 Protected Characteristics

The company prohibits discrimination and harassment based on any legally protected characteristic, including:

  • Race, color, and national origin or ancestry
  • Sex and sexual orientation
  • Gender identity and gender expression
  • Religion or creed
  • Age (40 and older, as protected by the ADEA)
  • Physical or mental disability
  • Veteran or military status
  • Pregnancy, childbirth, or related medical conditions
  • Genetic information
  • Marital status (where protected by state law)

3. Reporting Process

Employees who experience or witness conduct that may violate this Code of Conduct are strongly encouraged to report it. The company provides multiple reporting channels to accommodate different comfort levels:

  • Direct manager: If the situation does not involve your manager and you feel comfortable doing so, you may report to your direct manager first.
  • HR team: Send a written report to conduct@company.com. Reports are received by the HR Business Partner team and treated confidentially.
  • Anonymous hotline: Call or text 1-800-555-SAFE (1-800-555-7233), available 24/7. Reports are logged and routed to HR without identifying the caller. You may also submit anonymously at safereport.company.com.

Upon receipt of a report, HR will acknowledge receipt within 2 business days and commence a formal investigation within 5 business days. The investigation timeline varies depending on complexity; HR will provide status updates at least every 10 business days until the investigation is closed.

Note: You do not need to be certain that a policy violation occurred to make a report. If you witness something that makes you uncomfortable or seems inconsistent with our values, report it. HR will determine whether a formal investigation is warranted.

4. Anti-Retaliation Policy

Retaliation against any employee who, in good faith, reports a potential Code of Conduct violation, cooperates with an investigation, or exercises any other legally protected right is strictly prohibited. Retaliation includes any adverse employment action — including demotion, reduced hours, negative performance review, exclusion from meetings, hostile treatment, or termination — taken because of the employee's protected activity.

Retaliation is itself a terminable offense, separate from whatever underlying conduct was reported. An employee found to have retaliated against a reporter will be subject to immediate disciplinary action up to and including termination, regardless of whether the original report was substantiated.

If you believe you are experiencing retaliation, report it immediately through any of the reporting channels listed in Section 3. Retaliation reports are investigated with the same urgency as the original conduct complaint.

5. Disciplinary Process

Violations of this Code of Conduct are addressed through a progressive disciplinary process. The standard sequence is:

  1. Verbal warning — documented by the manager in the employee's HR file; employee acknowledges receipt
  2. Written warning — formal written notice identifying the policy violated, expected corrective action, and timeline; HR must be involved from this stage onward
  3. Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) — structured 30–90 day improvement plan with measurable goals; co-developed by manager and HR
  4. Termination — if the violation is not remediated or if the conduct is severe enough to warrant immediate action

The company reserves the right to skip one or more steps of this process when the severity of the violation warrants it. Zero-tolerance violations (harassment, violence, discrimination, retaliation) may result in immediate termination without prior warning. HR must be consulted before any termination decision is finalized.

6. Confidentiality and Record Retention

All conduct investigations are kept as confidential as possible, consistent with the company's obligation to conduct a thorough investigation. Information is shared only on a need-to-know basis with those directly involved in the investigation (e.g., the investigator, HR Business Partner, Legal counsel, and relevant managers).

Both the reporting party and the respondent will be notified of the investigation's outcome to the extent permitted by applicable law and consistent with the privacy of all parties involved. The outcome notice will state whether the complaint was substantiated, unsubstantiated, or inconclusive, and what corrective action (if any) was taken.

Investigation records — including the complaint, interview notes, evidence, and outcome — are retained in a secure HR file for a minimum of 7 years from the date the investigation is closed, in compliance with applicable record retention requirements.

7. Conflicts of Interest

Employees are required to disclose actual or potential conflicts of interest to HR and their manager promptly. Conflicts of interest include, but are not limited to:

  • Romantic or personal relationships with direct reports: Employees in a management or supervisory relationship with a direct or indirect report with whom they have or develop a romantic or sexual relationship must disclose this relationship to HR immediately. HR will assess whether a reporting restructure or other accommodation is required.
  • Vendor or supplier relationships: Employees who have a financial interest in, or a close personal relationship with, a current or prospective vendor or supplier must disclose this before participating in any procurement, contract, or vendor selection process involving that party.
  • Outside employment: Employees who hold outside employment, consulting arrangements, or board seats must disclose these to HR if they conflict or may appear to conflict with company interests, involve a competitor, or require use of company resources or information.

Disclosures are submitted via the Conflicts of Interest Disclosure Form available in BambooHR. HR will review and respond within 10 business days with guidance on whether the activity is approved, approved with conditions, or prohibited.

8. Social Media Policy

Employees are free to maintain personal social media accounts and express personal opinions. However, the following guidelines apply when social media activity intersects with work:

  • Confidential information: Never share non-public company information, including financial results, product roadmap details, customer names or data, employee information, or ongoing legal matters, on any social media platform regardless of your privacy settings.
  • Personal opinions: When expressing opinions on topics related to the company, its competitors, or its industry, clearly identify that your views are personal and do not represent the company's position. A disclaimer such as "Views my own" does not automatically shield the company from liability if your post could reasonably be attributed to the company.
  • Company branding: Use of the company name, logo, trademarks, or product images in social media posts requires prior approval from the Marketing Communications team. Unauthorized use of company branding is prohibited.
  • Harassment online: The same standards that apply to in-person conduct apply online. Harassing colleagues, customers, or competitors on social media — even from personal accounts and outside working hours — may constitute a Code of Conduct violation.
